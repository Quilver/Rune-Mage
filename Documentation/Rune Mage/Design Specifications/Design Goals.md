## Verisimilitude first design

There are many different aspects for an appealing game (fun, immersion, emotional depth, creativity, competitiveness, mastery, etc), but as a player…

I find myself drawn to games that sell an appealing fantasy. I am looking for games that let you immerse yourself in a role

This means I enjoyed games such as MESBG but don’t like game such as Age of Sigmar, because one prioritises mechanics that emulate the nature of battles whereas the other focuses more on big numbers.

I want a game where decisions the player makes can be explained by the narrative of the scenario rather than the quirks of the systems.

## Thematic inspirations, The Wizard’s (Hero’s) Journey

1. Wizard of Earthsea, Bartimaeus, Wheel of Time (books): my main points of reference for wizards
2. Sacrifice (the game): for its massive spells
3. Heresy (Manhua, AKA Demons and Strangers) or **(Journey To Heaven, manhua)**: Technically a murim/wuxia but enough of the powers are like magic and the thematic nature is gorgeous

To summarise, magic can be flashy and destructive but it is an intellectual exercise. A magician on some level is trying to become a god (all knowing, all powerful, immortal).

A wizard’s journey comes in stages. Initially they must learn their craft and survive their ignorance (don’t blow themselves up) without falling to easy solutions (deal with the devil). This is followed by some successes where use their knowledge to defeat enemies and gain earthly power. It is after these initial successes where the wizard faces their greatest challenge; dealing with the responsibilities they now have challenges their moral foundations and puts them at risk of losing their humanity (the man becomes a machine). If they pass this test they gain a form of apotheosis and if they fail they fall to their arrogance (incapable of understanding anything outside themselves).



## Other Ideas

### Spells/runes

Magic:

Medium (how the spell hits its targets), Duration (How long does the spell last), Effect (What it does to its target)

Medium

1. Projectile: most attacks will be some for of projectile, will effect anything hit by it. types of projectile are beam, bolt, cone, radius, zap, shotgun
2. Zone: Places zone either around or in front of player, effects trigger on anything in the zone. Zone may or may not target player, (a player would be effected by walking through their own burning room but would not effected by their own earthquake. Zones can range from large (burning room) to small (landmine).
3. Ward/buff: Effect applied directly to player
4. Shield: place wall between player and facing direction (reflection, barrier, blackhole)
5. Touch: Applies only when player is directly on the target

In addition mediums need a parameter for duration

Effect

1. Damage
2. Healing
3. Pushback
4. Poison
5. Stun
6. Reflect/Block/Absorb projectile
7. Spawn secondary magic

Spells:

1. Raise zombies/skeletons/golem: provides bodyguard, useful against fast, aggressive enemies so you have time/space to cast
2. Illuminate: creates light to see in dark areas and reveals Illusory Wall (see dark souls) upon initial cast
3. Dispel: cast against enchantment to unlock puzzle to break enchantment (for example dispel poison swamp requires dispel but also information about the swamp)
4. Cloak of Shadows: move undetected by normal enemies but you cannot cast spells
5. Activate: activates environmental enchanted item (Magical portals to hub, or other ritual/environmental powers)
6. Speak with dead: can listen to a corpse’s last words
7. Vortex of destruction: summons an orb that lasts several minutes that does tremendous damage and can be moved about
8. Cloud Walk: Walk through the clouds
9. Summon outer entity: Floor turns into face that swallows enemies
